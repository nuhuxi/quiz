What guitar is that?

In the past 100 years, the guitar has risen from an acoustic rhythm instrument to a cultural icon. No one played "air clarinet" in the Big Band Era but modern "air guitar" competitions attract a global audience. And, they are a whole lot of fun!

Many factors have contributed to the guitar's rise: advances in technology and manufacturing to name two. In turn, better sounding, less expensive instruments inspired legions of artists. This quiz will introduce you to some of the instruments played by the artists who shaped the musical landscape with their guitars.

Leo Fender started the Fender Musical Instrument Company in the depths of the Great Depression. His first guitar, the Broadcaster later became the Telecaster which is still by artists such as Bruce Springsteen and Brad Paisley. Leo also manufactured the first electric bass guitar.





Innovative guitarist Les Paul designed the guitar that bears his name. He was a pioneer with his partner Mary Ford in the se of magnestic tape for multi-track recording. The Les Paul shot to stardom in the hands of Led Zeepelin's Jimmy Page and is still found on rock stages the world over.

What company manufactured it?







In 1902, Orville Gibson started the Gibson Guitar-Mandolin Mfg Co. Ltd. in Kalamazoo MI. Over the years Gibson made everything from mandolins to aircraft parts during WWII. The Les Paul was probably the most commercially successful guitar they made. Gibson Mandolins are prized today for their beauty and tone.



Eddie Van Halen rocked the music industry in the early 80's with his astounding guitar technique. He couldn't have done it without the innovation of the EVH guitar. A locking "nut" made it possible to use the "whammy bar" to bend notes like no one had done before. Oh, he had great hair too.

Who manufactured Eddie's guitar?







The Kramer Guitarcompany was founded in the late 1970s by Dennis Berardi, Gary Kramer and Phillip J. Petillo Ph.D., Master Luthier-Engineer to manufacture aluminum-necked guitars. Based in New Jersey, they produced guitars used by heavy metal and shred guitar band in the 80's.
